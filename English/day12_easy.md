## 📝 Sample Interview Answer

### Question: "Explain a statistical concept you use frequently."
**Answer:** "I often use **correlation analysis** in my work. For example, in my last internship, I wanted to understand if there was a relationship between customer satisfaction scores and repeat purchases. I calculated the correlation coefficient and found a strong positive correlation of 0.78. This helped the team focus on improving satisfaction to increase customer retention."

### Question: "How do you handle outliers in data?"
**Answer:** "Outliers can distort analysis results. First, I identify them using box plots or the IQR method. Then I investigate whether the outlier is a data entry error or a genuine value. If it is an error, I correct or remove it. If it is genuine, I decide whether to exclude it based on the analysis goal."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Numbers & Percentages
| Expression | Pattern | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Increase | increased by + % | "Sales increased by 15%." |
| Decrease | decreased by + % | "Costs decreased by 8%." |
| From...to... | from X to Y | "Revenue grew from $1M to $1.5M." |
| Compared to | compared to + noun | "Compared to last year, profits are up." |
| Twice as much | twice as...as | "We have twice as many customers as before." |

## 💡 Interview Tip
When asked to explain a statistical concept, **use a real-world example** related to business. This shows you can apply theory to practice. Instead of just defining "standard deviation," say: "I used standard deviation to measure the variability in monthly sales, which helped management set realistic targets."
