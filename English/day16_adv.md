# Day 16: Data Presentation Language (ডেটা উপস্থাপনার ভাষা)

## 🔤 Vocabulary for Describing Trends (ট্রেন্ড বর্ণনার শব্দ)
| English | Bangla | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Increase | বৃদ্ধি | "Sales increased by 15%." |
| Decrease | হ্রাস | "Costs decreased by 8%." |
| Rise / Rise sharply | বাড়া / তীব্রভাবে বাড়া | "There was a sharp rise in March." |
| Drop / Drop sharply | পড়া / তীব্রভাবে পড়া | "The numbers dropped sharply in April." |
| Fluctuate | ওঠানামা করা | "The exchange rate fluctuated all year." |
| Remain stable | স্থিতিশীল থাকা | "Expenses remained stable." |
| Peak at | সর্বোচ্চে পৌঁছানো | "Sales peaked at $2M in December." |
| Bottom out | সর্বনিম্নে পৌঁছানো | "The market bottomed out in January." |
| Recover | পুনরুদ্ধার করা | "The economy started recovering in Q3." |
| Plateau | সমতল হওয়া | "Growth plateaued after the initial surge." |

## 📝 Describing Charts & Graphs

### Line Graph Vocabulary
- "There was an **upward trend** from January to June."
- "We saw a **downward trend** in the last quarter."
- "The line **remained flat** during the summer months."
- "There was a **dramatic increase** in November."
- "The trend **reversed direction** in September."

### Bar Chart Vocabulary
- "The highest value was in the Toronto region."
- "Montreal had the **lowest** sales."
- "Region A **outperformed** Region B by 25%."
- "There is a **significant gap** between the top and bottom performers."
- "The chart **compares** revenue across five product categories."

### Pie Chart Vocabulary
- "The largest **segment** is Product A at 40%."
- "Product B accounts for 25% of total revenue."
- "The remaining 35% is split between three products."
- "This chart shows the **percentage breakdown** of our costs."

## 📝 Sample Description
**Question:** "What does this chart tell us?"

**Answer:** "This line chart shows our monthly website traffic over the past year. Overall, there is a clear upward trend. Traffic increased steadily from January to June, with a sharp rise in March, likely due to the marketing campaign. There was a brief decline in July, but traffic recovered quickly and peaked in November at 100,000 visitors. December saw a slight drop, which is normal for the holiday season. The key takeaway is that our traffic has doubled compared to the beginning of the year."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Comparative & Superlative
Use comparatives to compare two things, superlatives for extremes.

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| high | higher | the highest |
| low | lower | the lowest |
| large | larger | the largest |
| small | smaller | the smallest |
| significant | more significant | the most significant |

**Examples:**
- "Toronto had **higher** sales than Montreal."
- "December had **the highest** revenue of all months."
- "This region is **more profitable** than that one."
- "This was **the most significant** finding in the report."

## 💡 Interview Tip
When asked to interpret a chart in an interview, follow this structure:
1. **Overall trend** — "Overall, sales are increasing."
2. **Key data points** — "The peak was in December."
3. **Anomalies** — "The drop in July is unusual."
4. **Conclusion** — "This suggests our strategy is working."
