## 📝 Small Talk Topics for Networking

### Weather / General
- "It is very cold today, isn't it?"
- "How is your week going?"
- "I love this coffee shop. Have you been here before?"

### Professional
- "What do you do in the data analytics field?"
- "What tools do you use most in your work?"
- "How did you get started in data analytics?"
- "What trends are you seeing in the industry?"

### About Canada (for newcomers)
- "How long have you been in Canada?"
- "What do you think of the Canadian winter?"
- "Which city do you prefer — Toronto or Vancouver?"
- "I am learning a lot about the Canadian job market."

## 📝 Networking Conversation
**You:** "Hi, my name is Jahirul. I am a finance graduate transitioning into data analytics."
**Person:** "Nice to meet you, Jahirul. I am Sarah. I work as a Data Analyst at RBC."
**You:** "That is great! I am very interested in the banking sector. What tools do you use at RBC?"
**Person:** "We mainly use SQL and Tableau. Python is becoming more popular too."
**You:** "I have experience with SQL and I am learning Python. Do you have any advice for someone entering the field?"
**Person:** "Definitely. I would suggest working on real projects and sharing them on GitHub."
**You:** "That is very helpful. Thank you, Sarah. Would you be open to connecting on LinkedIn?"

## 📝 Follow-up After Networking

## 📖 Grammar Point: Question Tags (ট্যাগ প্রশ্ন)
Question tags make conversations more natural.

| Statement | Tag | Example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| Positive → | Negative tag | "You work with data, **don't you?**" |
| Negative → | Positive tag | "You haven't used Tableau, **have you?**" |

**Common tags:** isn't it?, don't you?, haven't you?, aren't you?, didn't you?

**Examples:**
- "It is a nice day, **isn't it?**"
- "You have experience with SQL, **don't you?**"
- "That sounds interesting, **doesn't it?**"

## 💡 Interview Tip
**Networking is the #1 way to find a job in Canada.** Attend meetups, join LinkedIn groups, and reach out to people. Most jobs are not advertised publicly. When networking, focus on building genuine relationships, not just asking for a job.
