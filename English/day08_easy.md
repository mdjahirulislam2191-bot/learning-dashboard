## ⭐ What is the STAR Method?
The STAR method is a structured way to answer **behavioral interview questions** (আচরণগত প্রশ্ন). It helps you give clear, complete answers.

| Letter | Stands For | Bangla | What to Say |
|--------|------------|--------|-------------|
| **S** | **Situation** | পরিস্থিতি | Set the context. When? Where? Who? |
| **T** | **Task** | কাজ | What was your responsibility? |
| **A** | **Action** | পদক্ষেপ | What did YOU specifically do? |
| **R** | **Result** | ফলাফল | What happened? Use numbers if possible. |

## 📝 Example STAR Story

### Question: "Tell me about a time you solved a problem with data."

**S - Situation:**
"In my last job at XYZ Financial, our team was struggling with inaccurate sales reports. The reports were taking 3 days to prepare each week."

**T - Task:**
"My manager asked me to find a way to automate the reporting process and improve accuracy."

**A - Action:**
"I analyzed the existing process and identified the main issues: manual data entry errors and inconsistent formatting. I created an Excel macro to automate data cleaning and built a template report with formulas. I also wrote SQL queries to pull data directly from the database instead of manual entry."

**R - Result:**
"The new system reduced report preparation time from 3 days to 4 hours — a 90% improvement. Accuracy improved from 85% to 99%. My manager was very pleased and asked me to train the rest of the team."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Past Perfect for Background
Use **past perfect** (had + V3) to set up the situation.

| Tense | Use | Example |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Past Perfect (had V3) | Background before the story | "The team **had been struggling** for weeks." |
| Past Simple | Main events | "I **created** a new system." |
| Past Simple | Result | "The process **became** more efficient." |

## 💡 Interview Tip
**Always include numbers in your Result.** Numbers make your story more credible and memorable. 
- "Accuracy improved **by 15%**."
- "I saved **10 hours per week**."
- "Revenue increased **by $50,000**."

2. Write it using the STAR format (S, T, A, R).
