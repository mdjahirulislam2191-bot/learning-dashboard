## 📝 Conversation Practice (কথোপকথন অনুশীলন)

### Dialogue: Talking About Work History
**Interviewer:** "Could you walk me through your work experience?"
**You:** "Yes, of course. After graduating, I worked as a Junior Analyst at XYZ Financial Services for one year. My main responsibilities included preparing weekly reports, maintaining databases, and assisting the senior analysts."
**Interviewer:** "What kind of data did you work with?"
**You:** "I mainly worked with sales data and customer information. I used Excel to clean and organize the data before creating reports."

### Useful Phrases:
- "I worked for [company] as a [position]."
- "I was responsible for..."
- "My day-to-day tasks included..."
- "I gained experience in..."
- "One of my key achievements was..."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Present Perfect for Experience
Use **present perfect** to talk about experiences without specific time.

| Subject | Have/Has | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|----------|-----------------|---------|
| I | have | worked | "I have worked with Excel for three years." |
| I | have | used | "I have used SQL in several projects." |
| I | have | created | "I have created dashboards for management." |
| She | has | analyzed | "She has analyzed customer data." |

**Compare:**
- Present perfect: "I have worked with SQL." (general experience)
- Past simple: "I worked with SQL at my last job." (specific past job)

## 💡 Interview Tip
If you don't have direct Data Analyst experience, **focus on transferable skills**:
- "Although my title was not 'Data Analyst,' my work involved a lot of data analysis."
- "I created reports using Excel, which is similar to what Data Analysts do."
