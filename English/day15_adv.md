## 📝 Presentation Structure
A good data presentation has 3 parts:

### 1. Opening (শুরু)
**What to say:**
- "Good morning, everyone. Thank you for coming."
- "Today, I will present my analysis of..."
- "My presentation will cover three main areas."
- "I will take questions at the end."

### 2. Body (মূল অংশ)
**Transition phrases:**
- "Let's move to the first point."
- "Now, looking at this chart..."
- "As you can see from the data..."
- "This brings me to my next point."

**Describing data visuals:**
- "This bar chart compares sales across regions."
- "The line graph shows the trend over 12 months."
- "The pie chart breaks down our customer segments."
- "As highlighted in red, there was a significant drop."

### 3. Closing (শেষ)
**What to say:**
- "In conclusion, the data shows that..."
- "To summarize the key findings..."
- "Based on this analysis, I recommend..."
- "Thank you for your attention. I am happy to take questions."

## 📝 Sample Data Presentation Script
**You:** "Good morning. Today, I will present my analysis of our Q2 sales performance. This presentation has three parts: overall performance, regional breakdown, and recommendations.

Let's start with overall performance. As you can see in this line graph, sales increased by 12% compared to Q1. This was mainly driven by our Toronto and Vancouver offices.

Now, looking at the regional breakdown... [click to next slide] Our Toronto office saw a 20% increase, while Vancouver grew by 15%. However, the Montreal office declined by 5%.

Based on this data, I have two recommendations. First, we should invest more in the Toronto market. Second, we need to investigate the Montreal decline. I have attached a detailed report for your reference.

Thank you for listening. I am happy to answer any questions."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Signposting Language
Signposting helps your audience follow your presentation.

| Signpost | Use |
|----------|-----|
| "First / Second / Finally" | Listing points |
| "However / On the other hand" | Contrast |
| "Moreover / In addition" | Adding information |
| "Therefore / As a result" | Showing results |
| "For example / For instance" | Giving examples |
| "In other words" | Explaining differently |
| "In short / To summarize" | Concluding |

## 💡 Interview Tip
If asked to give a presentation during an interview:
1. **Know your audience** — technical or non-technical?
2. **Use fewer slides** — 5-7 slides max for a 15-min presentation
3. **Tell a story** — start with a question, show the data, end with insights
4. **Practice timing** — never go over your allotted time
