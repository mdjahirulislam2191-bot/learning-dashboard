## 📝 Case Study Framework

When given a case study, follow this structure:

### Step 1: Clarify (স্পষ্ট করা)
Ask questions to understand the problem.
- "What is the business problem we are trying to solve?"
- "What data is available?"
- "What is the deadline?"
- "Who is the audience for the recommendation?"

### Step 2: Structure (গঠন করা)
Outline your approach.
- "First, I will clean and explore the data."
- "Then, I will look for patterns and trends."
- "Finally, I will make a recommendation."

### Step 3: Analyze (বিশ্লেষণ)
Walk through your analysis step by step.
- State assumptions clearly.
- Show your work (SQL queries, Excel formulas, Python code).
- Interpret the results as you go.

### Step 4: Conclude (উপসংহার)
Present your findings.
- Key insight
- Recommendation
- Limitations
- Next steps

## 📝 Sample Case Study: Customer Churn Analysis

**Scenario:** "Our company has seen a 10% drop in customer retention. You have customer data for the last 12 months. What would you do?"

**Your Answer Structure:**

**Clarify:** "Before I start, I would like to clarify: do we have data on customer demographics, support interactions, payment history, and usage patterns? What is the time frame?"

**Structure:** "I would approach this in three steps. First, I will explore the data to find patterns. Second, I will identify the key factors driving churn. Third, I will recommend specific actions."

**Analysis:** 
"Let me walk through my analysis. First, I check the churn rate by customer segment. I notice that customers who contact support more than 3 times in a month have a 40% churn rate — that is 4 times higher than average.

Second, I look at payment method. Customers on monthly plans churn 2x more than annual subscribers.

Finally, I check usage frequency. Customers who use the product less than 5 times per week are most likely to leave."

**Conclusion:**
"My key finding is that poor support experience and low usage are the main drivers of churn. I recommend: (1) improve response time for support tickets, (2) encourage annual subscriptions with discounts, and (3) create an onboarding program to increase usage in the first 30 days.

One limitation is that I only have 12 months of data. To be more confident, I would recommend analyzing 2-3 years of data."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Conditional Sentences in Case Studies
| Type | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Zero conditional | General truth | "If churn is high, we lose revenue." |
| First conditional | Possible future | "If we improve support, churn will decrease." |
| Second conditional | Hypothetical | "If we had more data, we could run a deeper analysis." |

## 💡 Interview Tip
**In case study interviews, thinking out loud is essential.** The interviewer wants to see your thought process. Even if your answer is not perfect, showing structured thinking is more important. Say things like:
- "Let me think through this..."
- "One approach could be..."
- "Another factor to consider is..."
