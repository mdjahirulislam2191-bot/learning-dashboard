## 📝 Communicating with Executives: Key Principles

### 1. Start with the Bottom Line
Executives are busy. Tell them the conclusion FIRST, then explain if needed.

**BAD:** "I analyzed the data, cleaned it, ran correlations, and found that..."
**GOOD:** "Our customer retention rate has dropped by 15%. This is the most important finding."

### 2. Use Business Language, Not Technical Jargon
| Technical (Avoid) | Business-friendly (Use) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| "I ran a multiple linear regression." | "I analyzed what factors drive sales." |
| "The p-value was 0.02." | "The result is statistically significant." |
| "I optimized the ETL pipeline." | "I improved how we process data." |
| "The R-squared is 0.85." | "Our model explains 85% of the variation." |

### 3. Be Concise (সংক্ষিপ্ত)
Use the **BLUF** method: Bottom Line Up Front.

**Sample:**
"Good morning. Our Q3 revenue increased by 12%, exceeding our target. The key driver was our Toronto office. I recommend we invest more there. I have the detailed analysis ready if you have questions."

### 4. Support with Visuals, Not Raw Data
- Use charts, not tables with numbers
- Highlight the key insight in the chart
- Use red/green to show problems/opportunities

## 📝 Executive Presentation Sample
**You (to CEO):** "Good morning. I have completed the analysis on our customer churn issue. The bottom line is: we are losing 8% of customers each quarter, and this is costing us approximately $500,000 annually.

The main reason is customer service response time. When customers wait more than 24 hours for support, they are 3 times more likely to leave.

My recommendation is to increase the customer service team by 2 people. The cost is $80,000, but it would save us $500,000 in lost revenue. I have a detailed cost-benefit analysis if you would like to review it."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Reported Speech (পরোক্ষ উক্তি)
When reporting what an executive said:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "Show me the data." | "The CEO asked me to show the data." |
| "You are doing well." | "The CFO said I was doing well." |
| "I need this by Friday." | "The executive said they needed it by Friday." |

**Changes in reported speech:**
- Present → Past: "need" → "needed"
- Will → Would: "will review" → "would review"
- Can → Could: "can present" → "could present"

## 💡 Interview Tip
When interviewing for a Data Analyst role, show that you understand **business impact**. Don't just say "I know SQL." Say "I use SQL to find insights that save the company money." This is C-level thinking.
