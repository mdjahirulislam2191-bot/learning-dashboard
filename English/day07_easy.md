## 📝 TOP 10 Common Interview Questions

### 1. "Tell me about yourself."
**Bangla:** নিজের সম্পর্কে বলুন।
**Answer Framework:** Present → Past → Future
- Present: "I am a finance graduate with strong data analysis skills."
- Past: "I have worked with Excel and SQL for the past two years."
- Future: "I am looking to apply my skills in a Data Analyst role."

### 2. "Why do you want to work here?"
**Bangla:** আপনি কেন এখানে কাজ করতে চান?
**Strategy:** Research the company first. Mention specific things.
**Answer:** "I admire how your company uses data to drive decisions. I read your recent case study on customer analytics and I want to contribute to similar projects."

### 3. "What are your salary expectations?"
**Bangla:** আপনার বেতনের প্রত্যাশা কী?
**Answer:** "Based on my research and experience, I am looking for a range between $45,000 and $55,000 annually. However, I am open to discussing this further."

### 4. "What is your greatest weakness?"
**Bangla:** আপনার সবচেয়ে বড় দুর্বলতা কী?
**Formula:** Weakness + what you are doing to improve
**Answer:** "I sometimes spend too much time perfecting a report. I am learning to set time limits and prioritize completion over perfection."

### 5. "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
**Bangla:** ৫ বছরে নিজেকে কোথায় দেখেন?
**Answer:** "In 5 years, I see myself as a senior data analyst who can lead projects and mentor junior team members. I want to grow with a company like yours."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Conditional Sentences (If...)
Used for hypothetical questions.

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Real (likely) | If + present, will + verb | "If I get this job, I will work hard." |
| Hypothetical | If + past, would + verb | "If I had more experience, I would apply for senior roles." |
| Past unreal | If + had + V3, would have + V3 | "If I had studied Python earlier, I would have been more prepared." |

## 💡 Interview Tip
**Prepare 3-5 stories before the interview.** Most questions can be answered with a story. Keep them structured: Situation → Action → Result. More on this tomorrow!
