## 📝 Useful Phrases for Meetings

### Starting a Meeting
- "Good morning, everyone. Thank you for joining."
- "Let's get started."
- "The purpose of this meeting is to discuss..."
- "I have prepared an agenda for today."

### Asking Questions
- "Could you please clarify that point?"
- "I have a question about..."
- "Would you mind explaining that in more detail?"
- "Could you show me the data behind that?"

### Giving Opinions
- "In my opinion..."
- "From my perspective..."
- "Based on my analysis..."
- "I believe the data shows that..."

### Agreeing & Disagreeing
- "I agree with that point."
- "That makes sense."
- "I see your point, but..."
- "I respectfully disagree. The data suggests otherwise."

### Ending a Meeting
- "Thank you all for your time."
- "Let me summarize what we discussed."
- "I will send a follow-up email with action items."
- "Looking forward to our next meeting."

## 📝 Meeting Dialogue
**Manager:** "Good morning, team. Let's start. Does anyone have an update on the Q2 sales analysis?"
**You:** "Yes, I have completed the preliminary analysis. The data shows a 10% increase in Q2 compared to Q1. I can share the dashboard with everyone."
**Manager:** "That's great. What do you think is driving the growth?"
**You:** "Based on my analysis, the new marketing campaign is the main driver. There is a strong correlation between campaign spend and sales in the last two months."

## 📖 Grammar Point: Modal Verbs for Politeness
| Modal | Use | Example |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Could | Polite request | "Could you send me the data?" |
| Would | Polite offer/question | "Would you like me to present?" |
| May | Permission (formal) | "May I share my screen?" |
| Might | Possibility | "We might need more time to analyze this." |
| Should | Recommendation | "We should look at the outliers." |

## 💡 Interview Tip
**In Canadian meetings, being polite and collaborative is very important.** Do not interrupt others. Say "Excuse me" or "May I add something here?" when you want to speak. Listen more than you talk in your first few meetings.
